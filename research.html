A brief history

I’m doing research for quite a time1—and it has origins in the bachelor years. In general, my line of research has been (biology  ∨  medicine)  ∧  (computation  ∨  engineering), although it has changed along the years heading to the cellular level (biology) and its internal activities (computation) instead of organs (medicine) and healing supportive machines development (engineering).

These changes of interest and work are related to a series of environmental changes that has happened to me, but I’m still curious about all the subjects I’ve researched. If you want to contact me to discuss on any of these subjects, feel free and welcome.

To present my research, I’ve decided by the reverse chronological order. Although I think it may seems obvious for most of people, I still feel the necessity to explain the reasons:

  1. I’m (temporally) closer to the first subjects that appear, what means that I’m probably still working on them or have all the details clear in my mind.
  2. My newer works are more mature ones or those I would appreciate people’s commentaries on.
  3. I imagine people are more interest in my recent works than older ones.

Description

My PhD research project (proposal) seeks to find the existing relation between MP systems, reactive systems and electrical circuits. Some of the questions the research address are:

  • Is there a transformation ξ that maps a MP system in a circuit?
  • Is there a transformation ζ that maps a circuit in a MP system?
  • What is the class of circuits accepted for the transformation? Analog, digital or both?
  • What is the relation between ξ and ζ? Is ξ − 1 = ζ?
  • Is there a particular class of MP systems that can be transformed by ξ?
  • Is there a particular class of circuits that can be transfoed by ζ?

There are a number of relevant results derived from this research line; the main one is cell-on-a-chip or a simulation of a whole cell behaviour in a single unit of hardware. Other important results are:

  • a new language (MP grammar) for hardware specification;
  • possibility to work with one model expressed in a domain—let us say an digital circuit—in another one—in this case, MP system—, perform operation (such as optimization, reduction, etc) and, then, return to the original model domain;
  • automatic composition of hardware through definition of input signals, output signals and some known rules.2

This work is still being developed. In case you want to discuss it , have an insight or is just curiouos, please feel free to contact me.
